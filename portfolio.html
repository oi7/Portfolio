Ant-Based Modeling - Agent-Based City Simulation

Ongoing Starting from April 2016, Cambridge, Massachusetts
Research MIT Media Lab, Changing Places Group, Prof. Kent Larson
Poseidon Ho, Carson Smuts

Ant-Based Modeling is a new method of agent-based city simulation using living creatures (ants) and physical models (LEGOs). We are inspired by ant's perception and social behavior, and experimenting how different species of ants react to different environmental factors such as electromagnetic spectrum, magnetic fields and electric fields.
• Experimenting on electromagnetic spectrum, magnetic fields and electric fields
• Studying different species of ant’s sensing, perception, and social behaviors
• Built multiple LEGO cities for ants

View details

OpenScope - Crowdsourcing for Mobile Microscopy

Ongoing Starting from January 2016, Cambridge, Massachusetts
Research MIT Media Lab, Civic Media Group, Prof. Ethan Zuckerman
Poseidon Hai-Chi Ho, Nickolaos Savidis

OpenScope is an open source project that combines three components for anyone to explore the micro world anytime, anywhere. The 3D-printable open hardware turns your smartphone into a 200x microscope, the image processing application helps you recognize specific objects, and the online community allows you to share and contribute your findings from the microscope. OpenScope is expanding microscopy technologies beyond research laboratories and transforming the way we interact with the micro world.
• Developed mobile application with custom training models
• Co-designed the mechanism of the 3D-printable hardware kit

Alan - Multimodal Interactive Robot

Completed November 2014, Beijing, China
Team Poseidon Ho, Zijun Jia, Vincent Chen

Alan is an multimodal interactive robot named after "Alan Turing". It is Turing Robot's first physical robot, demonstrating its strengths in Multimodal Interactions and Chinese Natural Language Processing. Alan targets at 6-8 years children in China.

• Industrial Design: Prototyped the production models for “Alan”, coordinating with Beluga Lab
