MoboWater - Crowdsensing platform for water properties

Ongoing Starting from January 2016, Cambridge, Massachusetts
Research MIT Center for Civic Media

MoboWater is a crowdsensing platform that combines 3 components for anyone to conduct instantaneous water sensing anytime, anywhere. The hardware kit, attachable to mobile devices, includes a 200x micro lens to make water turbidity and bacteria visible. The mobile application embeds custom training models to perform image processing for analyzing water purity and recognizing specific bacterias. The web application visualizes water properties into location-based open datasets, which is designed for citizens and communities to create campaigns with civic engagement. MoboWater is expanding water sensing technologies from research laboratories and transforming the way citizens evaluate and interact with their water supplies.
• Developed Hardware Kit, Mobile Application and Web Application.

Alan - Emotional Interactive Robot

Completed November 2014, Beijing, China
Team Poseidon, Zijun from Turing Robot, Vincent from Beluga Lab

Alan is an emotional interactive robot named after "Alan Turing" by Turing Robot. It is Turing Robot's first physical robot, demonstrating its strengths in Multi-modal Interactions and Chinese Natural Language Processing. Alan targets at 6-8 years old children in China around 1,500 RMB. Poseidon took major responsibilities in designing the interactions and emotions of Alan.
• Interaction Design: Designed 88 sets of actions and expressions, 120 sets of voices and sounds
• Emotional Design: Designed 25 language emotions for robot based on Chinese NLP
• Industrial Design: Prototyped the production models for “Alan”, coordinating with Beluga Lab
